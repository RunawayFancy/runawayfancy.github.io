Research

Quantum Physics

A quantum algorithm for collision inducing disturbance vectors in SHA-1

Advisor: Prof. Hou IAN

Seeding a disturbance vector in the has mapping to obtain a successful collision is a major focus of cryptographic study in the past two decades to improve hash protocols. We propose an algorithm that takes advantage of entangled quantum states for concurrent seeding of candidate disturbance vectors, out of which the one entailing collision is selected thought a combination of Grover search , phase-gate, and information feedbacks form classical computing machinery. The complexity reduction is shown to be on the order of $O(\sqrt{2^{n_2}})$ where $n_2$ is the number of qubits encoding addresses. We demonstrate the practicality of the proposed by an implementation scheme based on degenerate optical parametric oscillators.

Review MAX-CUT problem with 2000-node optimization by simulated and quantum annealing algorithms

Advisor: Prof. Hou IAN

A summer research project finished in June, 2021. We review using simulated annealing and quantum annealing algorithms to minimize the Hamiltonian of an Ising spin glass model to solve a corresponding MAX-CUT problem. We compared the efficiency between homogeneous and inhomogeneous simulated annealing based on the logarithm annealing schedule. The final report is available here.

Diagonalizing the Hamiltonian of two coupled flux qubits for realizing a strong field electromagnetically induced transparency

Advisor: Ziyang YOU

I helped to diagonalize the Hamiltonian of two $\sigma_x$ coupled flux qubits which aim to realizing a strong field electromagnetic induced transparency. The detailed file is available here.
